The problem you described

NFL isn't player-vs-player — everything has context. You laid it out in four layers. Here's your framing, kept intact:

Layer 1
Game

Odds, spread, moneyline, totals, records, home/away, weather, stadium, rest days, prime-time.

Layer 2
Team

Off/def rankings, pace, run-pass tendency, red-zone, third-down, turnover diff, injuries, recent form.

Layer 3
Player

Projected starters, injury status, snap counts, usage trends, historical performance, matchup history, props.

Layer 4
Matchup

Team-vs-team history, QB-vs-defense, WR-vs-secondary, RB-vs-run-D, O-line-vs-pass-rush, coaching.

The solution, in one line

One Postgres database you own, fed on schedule by four sources, where the deepest layer — the matchups — is calculated inside the engine by joining everything else. That computation is the intelligence.

The database that holds it together

Every source writes into one Postgres schema keyed on stable IDs. The betting market from scores24 joins onto SofaScore's teams; nflverse history joins onto the same players. The result: any upcoming game resolves — in a single query — to its odds, both teams' records & form, the head-to-head, and every computed matchup edge, with nothing left dangling.

How each signal arrives

Three delivery modes — and the middle one is where the value lives.

Sourced

Pulled from a feed

Facts that exist somewhere — odds, records, snap counts, rosters. We fetch, clean and key them.

Computed

Calculated in-engine

Signals no site publishes — "this QB vs this defense." We derive them by joining the layers. This is the edge.

v2

Enriched later

Player props need a paid market feed. v1 already builds the data to predict them; v2 adds the market line to compare against.

Honest scope — what waits for v2

Player props are the one item held for phase two. No source in v1 carries them, and the feeds that do charge a monthly subscription. But nothing is lost: v1 builds the complete foundation — per-game history, snaps, usage, matchup features — to project player output ourselves. In v2 we add the market's prop line and the engine flags where the two disagree. That comparison is the payoff, and it only needs a number bolted on later.
